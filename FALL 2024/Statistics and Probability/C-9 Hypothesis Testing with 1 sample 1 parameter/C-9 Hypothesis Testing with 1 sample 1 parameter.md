    1. $H_0$ is the null hypothesis is always the equality
    2. $H_1$ is the alternative hypothesis is always $\neq$, <, or >

# Type 1 and Type 2 error

1. Type 1 error happens when we reject $H_0$ when $H_0$ is actually True
2. P(type 1 error) = $\alpha$
3. P(not type 1 error) = 1 - $\alpha$

  

![[image 1 18.png|image 1 18.png]]

  

# The P Value

The p value is the smallest level of significance, $\alpha$ that causes us to reject H_0

when the p - value > $\alpha$ , we always fail to reject

when the p - value < $\alpha$, we always reject H_0

  

  

# Types of hypo tests

\theta is any population parameter we are interested in

  

1. Two sided tests
    1. $H_0, \theta = \theta_0$
    2. $H_1, \theta \neq \theta_0$

  

1. One sided Upper
    1. $H_0, \theta = \theta_0$
    2. $H_1, \theta > \theta_0$

  

1. One sided lower
    1. $H_0, \theta = \theta_0$
    2. $H_1, \theta < \theta_0$

  

1. In one sided tests, rejecting null hypo is a **Strong conclusion**

  

# Two sided hyp test and two sided CI relationship

1. If $\theta_0$ lies in the two sided ci bounded by L and U, then we will fail to reject $H_0$ in a two sided tests.
